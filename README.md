# ed-concealment-computational-analysis
# Computational Analysis of Eating Disorder Concealment

## Overview

This computational literature review examines whether psychosocial factors associated with eating disorder concealment among males in Southeast Asia are interrelated. Using manually coded data from 50 peer-reviewed studies, the project combines exploratory data analysis, correlation analysis, and natural language processing (NLP) to identify patterns and relationships between concealment, stigma, shame, masculinity, family influence, and help-seeking.

## Research Question

Are psychosocial factors associated with eating disorder concealment among males in Southeast Asia related to one another, and what patterns emerge through computational analysis?

## Project Workflow

This project began with the collection of 50 research articles related to eating disorders among males in Southeast Asia. Relevant information from each study was manually extracted and organized into a structured dataset, including variables such as concealment, stigma, shame, masculinity, family influence, help-seeking behavior, prevalence, and measurement tools.

The dataset was analyzed using Python in Google Colab. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was performed to visualize theme frequencies, study characteristics, and relationships between variables. Correlation analysis was then used to examine how concealment-related psychosocial factors were associated with one another across the literature.

To complement the quantitative analysis, Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques were applied to the abstracts of the selected studies. This included text preprocessing, comparative bigram analysis, and Keyword-in-Context (KWIC) analysis to identify recurring themes and contextual relationships within the literature.

The findings from the statistical analysis and NLP were integrated to provide computational evidence of the relationships among factors associated with eating disorder concealment in males from Southeast Asia.
